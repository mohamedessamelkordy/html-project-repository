<h1 style="color:white;font-size:30px;font-family:verdana;">Artificial intelligence history</h1> 



<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">artificial beings first appeared in the ancient Greek myths of Antiquity.
The following is a quick look at some of the most important events in AI.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">1952: Arthur Samuel develops a self-learning program to play checkers.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">1963: John McCarthy starts the AI Lab at Stanford.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">1972: The logic programming language PROLOG is created.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">1991: U.S. forces deploy DART, an automated logistics planning and scheduling tool, during the Gulf War.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">1997: IBM's Deep Blue beats world chess champion Gary Kasparov</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">2005: STANLEY, a self-driving car, wins the DARPA Grand Challenge.

The U.S. military begins investing in autonomous robots like Boston Dynamic's "Big Dog" and iRobot's "PackBot."</p>
<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">2008: Google makes breakthroughs in speech recognition and introduces the feature in its iPhone app.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">2012: Andrew Ng, founder of the Google Brain Deep Learning project, feeds a neural network using deep learning algorithms 10 million YouTube videos as a training set. The neural network learned to recognize a cat without being told what a cat is, ushering in breakthrough era for neural networks and deep learning funding.</p>

<p style="color:white;font-size:20px;font-family:verdana;">2014: Google makes first self-driving car to pass a state driving test.
</p>
